Why most GTM plans fail

Strategy without execution is just a slide deck

Most go-to-market plans fail for the same handful of reasons: they're too generic, they lack a clearly defined ICP, they conflate channels with strategy, and nobody owns execution once the document is written. The plan looks thorough in a board meeting, then collects dust while the team improvises. The result is a slow, expensive launch that burns runway and momentum at the same time.

I've been part of multiple product launches and market entries across different industries and geographies — including e-commerce platforms, AdTech products, and brand partnerships at scale. I know what a realistic, executable GTM plan looks like, and I know how to drive it forward hands-on. I don't just hand you the strategy; I stay involved through the early execution phase to make sure it actually lands.
